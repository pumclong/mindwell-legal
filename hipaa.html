HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices

MindWell - AI Therapy Companion

Effective Date: September 15, 2025

THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW MEDICAL INFORMATION ABOUT YOU MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

1. Our Commitment to Your Privacy

MindWell is committed to protecting your health information. We are required by law to:

  • Maintain the privacy of your protected health information (PHI)
  • Provide you with this notice of our legal duties and privacy practices
  • Follow the terms of the notice currently in effect
  • Notify you if we are unable to agree to a requested restriction
  • Notify you of any breach of unsecured PHI

2. What is Protected Health Information (PHI)?

Your PHI includes any health information that can identify you, such as:

  • Therapy session conversations and transcripts
  • Mental health assessments and scores (PHQ-9, GAD-7, etc.)
  • Mood tracking data and progress metrics
  • Crisis intervention records
  • Treatment goals and therapeutic outcomes
  • Device usage patterns related to mental health

3. How We May Use and Disclose Your PHI

Treatment

We use your PHI to provide therapeutic services, including:

  • Generating personalized AI therapy responses
  • Tracking your mental health progress
  • Adapting treatment approaches to your needs
  • Providing crisis intervention when necessary

Payment

We may use your PHI for payment activities, such as:

  • Processing subscription payments
  • Coordinating with insurance providers (if applicable)
  • Billing for services rendered

Healthcare Operations

We may use your PHI for healthcare operations, including:

  • Quality assurance and improvement
  • Clinical oversight and supervision
  • Training of AI models (de-identified data only)
  • Compliance audits and reviews

4. Special Situations

Emergency Situations

We may disclose your PHI without your authorization in emergency situations involving:

  • Imminent danger to yourself or others
  • Threats of self-harm or suicide
  • Legal requirements to prevent harm
  • Court-ordered disclosures

Required by Law

We may disclose your PHI when required by federal, state, or local law, including:

  • Public health activities
  • Abuse, neglect, or domestic violence reporting
  • Health oversight activities
  • Judicial and administrative proceedings
  • Law enforcement purposes (limited circumstances)

5. Your Rights Regarding Your PHI

Right to Access

You have the right to inspect and obtain copies of your PHI that we maintain. To request access:

  • Submit a written request to privacy@mindwell-app.com
  • We will respond within 30 days
  • We may charge a reasonable fee for copying

Right to Amend

You have the right to request that we amend your PHI if you believe it is incorrect or incomplete:

  • Submit a written request with supporting documentation
  • We may deny your request if the information is accurate
  • You may submit a statement of disagreement

Right to Restrict Uses and Disclosures

You have the right to request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI:

  • We are not required to agree to all restrictions
  • If we agree, we will honor the restriction
  • Emergency situations may override restrictions

Right to Request Confidential Communications

You have the right to request that we communicate with you about your PHI in a certain way or at a certain location:

  • We will accommodate reasonable requests
  • You do not need to explain your reason
  • Submit requests to privacy@mindwell-app.com

Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to receive an accounting of disclosures we have made of your PHI:

  • Covers disclosures made in the six years prior to your request
  • Does not include disclosures for treatment, payment, or operations
  • First accounting in a 12-month period is free

Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You have the right to a paper copy of this notice:

  • Request a copy at privacy@mindwell-app.com
  • You may request a copy even if you agreed to electronic delivery

6. Security Safeguards

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: AES-256-GCM for data at rest, TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • Access Controls: Unique user authentication and authorization
  • Audit Logs: Comprehensive logging of all PHI access
  • Certificate Pinning: Secure API communications
  • Biometric Authentication: Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint

Administrative Safeguards

  • Privacy Officer: Designated privacy and security oversight
  • Workforce Training: HIPAA compliance training for all staff
  • Incident Response: Procedures for security incidents
  • Business Associates: HIPAA-compliant agreements with vendors

Physical Safeguards

  • Data Centers: Secure, monitored facilities
  • Device Security: Encrypted storage on mobile devices
  • Media Controls: Secure disposal of electronic media

7. Business Associates

We may share your PHI with business associates who help us provide services. These include:

  • Google LLC: AI processing services (Gemini API)
  • ElevenLabs: Voice synthesis services
  • Cloud hosting providers: Secure data storage

All business associates must sign agreements requiring them to protect your PHI according to HIPAA standards.

8. Data Retention and Disposal

  • Active Data: Retained while your account is active
  • Account Deletion: All PHI permanently deleted within 30 days
  • Crisis Records: Retained for 7 years per legal requirements
  • Audit Logs: Retained for 6 years for compliance
  • Secure Disposal: DoD 5220.22-M standard for data destruction

9. Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI, we will:

  • Notify you within 60 days of discovery
  • Report to the Department of Health and Human Services within 60 days
  • Provide details about what happened and what we're doing about it
  • Offer steps you can take to protect yourself

10. Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change this notice and make the new notice apply to PHI we already have as well as any PHI we receive in the future. We will:

  • Post the revised notice in the app
  • Notify users via email (if provided)
  • Make the notice available upon request
